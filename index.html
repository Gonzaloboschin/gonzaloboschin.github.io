About

My name is Gastón Emanuel Cisterna, and I am a Nuclear Engineer with a strong academic and practical background in nuclear technology. I earned a combined Bachelor’s and Master’s degree from Balseiro Institute , where I gained comprehensive training in reactor physics, instrumentation, and nuclear safety, alongside hands-on experience with the RA-6 research reactor. My final project focused on designing cold neutron guides for SANS instruments, contributing to the development of the RA-10 reactor. Later, through the Erasmus Mundus Joint Master Degree in Nuclear Physics, I specialized in quantum mechanics and detector technologies, culminating in a thesis on GN-Vision, a dual neutron-gamma imaging device for medical and waste treatment applications. I have always approached challenges with discipline and a focus on process over results, a mindset that has consistently led me to meaningful accomplishments. Whether working on complex research projects or personal goals, I value the small, consistent efforts that lead to impactful outcomes. This passion for problem-solving and my drive to contribute to sustainable innovations have shaped my journey in nuclear engineering. Currently, as a researcher at IFIC in Valencia, I am developing neutron detectors for medical applications while maintaining a deep interest in nuclear reactors and their fuel cycles. This blend of experience and determination defines how I plan to approach any opportunity or project that comes my way.
